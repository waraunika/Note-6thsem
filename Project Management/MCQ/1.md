# Melamchi Drinking Water Project: Detailed Summary

## Project Status

- **Total duration**: 42 years have passed, but Kathmandu residents still haven't received Melamchi water
- **Start date**: Government initiated Melamchi Drinking Water Project in 1998/1999 (2055 BS)
    
- **Current status**: Project claimed to be in final stage, but public trust remains low
    

---

## Tender History and Contractors

### Initial Phase (1998-2008)

- **2055 BS (1998/99)**: Melamchi Drinking Water Development Committee established
    
- **2058 BS (2001/02)**: Feasibility study with Italian company assistance
    
- **2062 BS (2005/06)**: Contract agreement with Italian company "Condotte"
    
- **2065 BS (2008/09)**: Construction began under Condotte
    
- **2068 BS (2011/12)**: Condotte failed to complete work, tunnel construction halted
    
- **Contract status**: Only 15% work completed in 3 years, contract terminated
    

### Second Phase (2008-2012)

- **2065 BS (2008/09)**: Again tender process
    
- **2068 BS (2011/12)**: Sino Hydro Corporation (Chinese company) awarded contract
    
- **Target**: Complete tunnel within 4 years
    
- **Reality**: Work delayed due to earthquake and border blockade
    

### Third Phase (2012-2019)

- **2070 BS (2013/14)**: Again tender process
    
- **2071 BS (2014/15)**: Sino Hydro awarded contract again
    
- **Target date**: March 2019 (2075 BS Phalgun) to complete work
    
- **Actual**: Contractor stopped work in Jestha 2075 (May/June 2018)
    
- **Current**: Cooperatives decided to terminate contract in Shrawan 2075 (July/August 2018)
    

---

## Cost and Expenditure

### Total Cost

- **Initial estimate**: 4.73 billion NPR when project began
    
- **Current expenditure**: Over 54 billion NPR spent in 42 years
    
- **Tunnel and distribution system alone**: Over 27 billion NPR
    
- **First phase only**: Approximately 16 billion NPR spent
    
- **Per liter cost**: Massive cost overrun compared to initial estimate
    

### Funding Sources

- **Italian company**: Loan assistance
    
- **Nepal government**: Investment
    
- **Asian Development Bank (ADB)**: Loan assistance
    
- **Current liability**: 3.12 billion NPR more than initial estimate
    

---

## Current Water Production and Demand

### Melamchi Treatment Plant

- **Current production capacity**: 13 million liters per day (MLD)
    
- **3 years ago capacity**: 8 MLD only
    
- **Increase**: Only 4 MLD increase in 3 years
    
- **Future target**: 17 MLD after new treatment method
    
- **Current operators**: 8 treatment plants of various types running
    

### Kathmandu Valley Demand-Supply Gap

- **Total demand**: 50 crore liters (500 million liters) per day
    
- **Current supply**: Only 30 crore liters (300 million liters) per day
    
- **Supply gap**: 40% deficit
    
- **Leakage/Wastage**: 40% of supplied water lost in distribution
    
- **Actual reaching homes**: Only 7 crore 50 lakh liters (75 million liters)
    

### Water Sources Currently Used

- **Bagnati River system**
    
- **Mahadev Khola, Dhobikhola**
    
- **Bishnumati, Balkhu, Manohara**
    
- **Kodku, Nakkhu, Godavari**
    
- **Bagmati, Hanumante, others**
    
- **Groundwater extraction**
    

---

## Construction Status

### Tunnel

- **Total tunnel length**: 27 km
    
- **Headrace tunnel**: Construction completed
    
- **7 intake structures**: Completed
    
- **Capacity**: 170 MLD design capacity
    

### Distribution Network

- **Pipeline installation**: Ongoing
    
- **Reservoir construction**: Various locations
    
- **Treatment plant**: Under construction/upgradation
    
- **Overall physical progress**: 85% claimed by officials
    

---

## Major Problems and Challenges

### Contract Management

- **Repeated tender cancellations**: Multiple contractor changes
    
- **Poor contractor evaluation**: Companies awarded contract without proper capacity assessment
    
- **Contract termination issues**: Legal complications
    
- **Time extensions**: Multiple deadline extensions
    

### Technical Challenges

- **Geological problems**: Difficult Himalayan terrain
    
- **Tunnel collapse**: During construction phase
    
- **Earthquake 2015**: Damaged structures
    
- **Border blockade 2015/16**: Construction materials stopped
    
- **Water leakage**: 40% water loss in distribution
    

### Financial Issues

- **Cost overrun**: 3.12 billion NPR excess
    
- **Interest burden**: On foreign loans
    
- **Budget management**: Poor allocation/utilization
    
- **Compensation**: Delay in land acquisition compensation

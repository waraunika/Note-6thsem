# 1. Project Overview

## Basic Information

- **Tunnel Name**: Nagdhunga-Naubise Tunnel
    
- **Significance**: **Nepal's FIRST-ever tunnel highway**
    
- **Status**: Tender announced for operation and maintenance
    
- **Current phase**: Pre-operation (Soft opening planned)
    
- **Location**: Nagdhunga-Naubise section (entrance to Kathmandu Valley)
    

## Strategic Importance

- This is Nepal's **first infrastructure project of its kind**
    
- Will set **precedent** for how future tunnels are managed
    
- Government's **learning ground** for tunnel O&M (Operation & Maintenance)
    
- **Model** for future PPP/toll road projects

# 3. Electricity Requirement

## Power Consumption

- **Daily consumption**: **2 Megawatts** (estimated)
    
- **Power supply**: **Dedicated line** proposed
    
- **Backup**: **Generators** mandatory
    
- **Status**: Part of tender specifications
    

## Significance

- Tunnel ventilation, lighting, monitoring systems require continuous power
    
- Dedicated line ensures uninterrupted operation
    
- Backup essential for emergency situations

# 6. Eligibility Criteria for Companies

## A. Joint Venture (JV) Requirement

### Mandatory Condition

- ✅ **Nepali company MUST be a JV partner**
    
- ✅ Maximum **3 companies** in JV
    
- ✅ At least **1 Nepali company** mandatory
    
- ✅ International company + Nepali company JV required
    

### Purpose

- **Technology transfer** to local companies
    
- **Knowledge/skill development** for Nepali firms
    
- **Capacity building** for future tunnel projects
    
- Government will **monitor** technology transfer compliance

# 7. Technology Transfer Mandate - CRITICAL PROVISION

## Why Mandatory?

- Nepal's **first tunnel** - no domestic expertise
    
- Government wants Nepali companies to **learn** from international experts
    
- Future tunnels (Fast Track, other projects) need local capability
    

## What is Required?

### 1. JV with Nepali Company

- International company **cannot bid alone**
    
- **Must form JV** with Nepali company
    
- Only **one Nepali company** mandatory (can be more)
    

### 2. Technology Transfer Plan

- Bidder must submit **detailed plan** for technology transfer
    
- **Regular schedule** of knowledge transfer activities
    
- **How** local manpower will be trained
    
- **What skills** will be transferred
    
- **Timeline** for each phase
    

### 3. Annual Assurance

- Company must **prove annually** that transfer is happening
    
- **Submit progress reports**
    
- Government will **monitor** compliance
    

### 4. Manpower Commitment

- **Both** local and international manpower must work throughout contract period
    
- No replacement of local staff with internationals only
    
- Ensures **hands-on learning**
    

---

## Consequence of Technology Transfer

- Nepali companies will gain **world-class tunnel O&M skills**
    
- Future tunnels can be operated by **domestic firms**
    
- Reduces **foreign dependency** in long term
    
- Creates **new expertise sector** in Nepal
